Shahvez Khan

Software Development Engineer, Google

From Classroom to Cloud: The Journey

Like many B.Tech CSE students at GLA University, Shahvez Khan started with a dream: to work at a top tech company and solve problems that impact millions. He was ambitious and diligent in his coursework, but he knew that landing a role at a company like Google required more than just good grades. It required a strategic, relentless approach to preparation.

The Grind: Mastering the Fundamentals

Shahvez dedicated himself to mastering the twin pillars of any top-tier technical interview: Data Structures & Algorithms (DSA)and System Design. His days were a disciplined mix of attending lectures, and then spending countless hours on platforms like LeetCode and GeeksforGeeks, solving problems and internalizing patterns. He understood that it wasn't about memorizing solutions, but about building a fundamental problem-solving intuition.

"You can't just know the theory. You have to live it, breathe it, and solve problems until the patterns become second nature. Consistency is everything."

The Prep Hub Advantage

While self-study built his foundation, Shahvez credits the **GLA Placement Prep Hub** as the catalyst that took his preparation to the next level. "The 'Verified Roadmaps' were a game-changer," he explains. "Instead of randomly solving problems, I had a clear, step-by-step guide curated by GLA seniors who had already cracked these interviews. It saved me months of guesswork."

He became a power user of the **AI Mock Interview** tool. "The AI was brutally honest," he laughs. "It gave me real-time feedback on not just the correctness of my answers, but my communication, pacing, and how clearly I articulated my thought process. It was like having an unlimited number of practice interviews with a real Google engineer."

Key Takeaways from Shahvez's Success

Shahvez's journey from a GLA classroom to a Google office wasn't magic; it was a result of a clear strategy and unwavering dedication. Here's his advice for aspiring students:

  • Start with a Strong DSA Foundation: This is non-negotiable. Spend at least 6-8 months getting comfortable with all major data structures and algorithms.
  • Simulate Real Interviews: Use tools like the Prep Hub's AI Mock Interviewer. The pressure is real, and you need to practice thinking on your feet.
  • Follow a Structured Path: Don't prepare blindly. Use a verified roadmap to guide your learning and ensure you cover all necessary topics for your target companies.
  • Never Stop Learning: Beyond DSA, develop a deep curiosity for core CS concepts like Operating Systems, Databases, and System Design. Read tech blogs and engineering write-ups from top companies.

His story is a powerful testament to the fact that with the right resources, a strong community, and an incredible amount of hard work, any GLA student can achieve their dream career.
